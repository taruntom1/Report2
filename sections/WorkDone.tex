\section{Discussion}
Overall, the simulation campaign gave consistent evidence of logical correctness, timing consistency, and
forwarding behavior under both nominal and adverse modeled conditions. It also gave us a reproducible baseline
that we could compare against the real-world measurements in this chapter.

Across the subsystems we tested, the field experiments confirmed four main behaviors: multi-hop delivery beyond
direct range, priority-consistent forwarding delay, directional relay suppression, and distributed best-relay
selection. During the evaluation period, we did not see routing loops, duplicate final deliveries, or spurious
relay activations. These results support decentralized operation for the tested topology and radio settings,
but wider generalization would need larger-scale mobility trials.
